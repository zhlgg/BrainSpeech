BrainSpeech: Decoupling Semantic Reasoning and Speech Generation for End-to-End Expressive Spoken Language Modeling


Abstract

Recent advances in Speech Language Models (SpeechLMs) have enabled end-to-end spoken dialogue systems. However, most existing SpeechLMs rely on a single-stream or sequential modeling paradigm, in which semantic reasoning and acoustic generation are either flattened into one stream or processed in a fixed Thinker–Talker order. This design implicitly assumes that thinking and speaking must be serialized, which limits the naturalness and expressiveness of spoken interaction. In this work, we revisit this assumption and propose BrainSpeech, a novel SpeechLM framework that models semantic reasoning and acoustic generation as two parallel yet interacting streams within a unified large language model. To enable effective cross-stream interaction, we introduce a dedicated attention mechanism that explicitly exchanges semantic and acoustic information during generation. Building on this dual-stream formulation, we further develop a three-stage training strategy that preserves the reasoning capability of the underlying language model while reducing the reliance on large-scale spoken dialogue corpora. In addition, we design a streaming decoding strategy that supports real-time generation of continuous, high-fidelity speech. Experiments on multiple spoken dialogue benchmarks demonstrate that BrainSpeech produces more natural and expressive speech and achieves superior speech-to-speech performance compared to larger open-source SpeechLMs.

Model Overview

The BrainSpeech that decouple semantic reasoning and acoustic realization with enhanced inference mechanism. The model utilizes a Whisper encoder to align input audio with the LLM, where text tokens are decoded independently to preserve semantic integrity, while audio tokens are predicted in parallel. To achieve dynamic emotion perception, we propose an expressive training phase that utilizes emotional TTS datasets. This strategy significantly improves implicit emotion perception capabilities, enables the model to handle broader scenarios, and enhances paralinguistic modeling without multimodal dialogue data. To achieve seamless and natural streaming synthesis, we introduce an overlapping-buffer technique that smooths inter-segment transitions via waveform splicing, alongside a Forced-Chunking method that standardizes processing latency through iterative batch accumulation.
